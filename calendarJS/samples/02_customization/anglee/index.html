JOURNEY TO FIND HIS HOMETOWN




“I'm a drifter and an outsider. There's not one single environment I can totally belong to”

Taiwanese born and America educated, Ang Lee fails to have a personal identity within either society. Lee's personal diaspora between two conflicting cultures is reflected in his films as they explore themes of self discovery within rigid social codes and parental pressures.

In The Wedding Banquet, Wai-Tung Gao battles between his parent's traditional Chinese beliefs and his own homosexual identifications.

In Hulk, Bruce attempts to control his anger to prevent his extermination that society desires and please his father.

In Brokeback Mountain, Jack and Ennis deny their love for each other due to societal pressures and unacceptance.

Along with exploring themes of diaspora within his films, Ang Lee utilizes a diverse crew on most films allowing various international influences.

In Sense and Sensibility, Ang Lee moves from Taiwainese films to British films, marking his first endeavor into international film industries.

British screenwriter and actress Emma Thompson remembers some miscommunications in the director-actor interactions stemming from the different film environments in both countries. After they “passed each other in the night unwittingly and pushed [apology] notes under each other’s doors” both adapted. Thompson fondly remembers the experience saying “we had the most wonderful time because his notes were so brutal and funny.”

Perhaps Ang Lee’s most recent film Life of Pi has the most multicultural influences. Ang Lee replaced Tobey Maguire within the film because he “decided to go with an entirely international cast”. The movie was filmed across the world in India, Taiwan and Canada. The 3D effects of the film were create in collaboration with artists in LA, India, Malaysia, Canada and Taiwan.

Ang Lee's return to Taiwan to film Life of Pi was particularly special to him. “It was very emotional for me filming Life of Pi in Taiwan. Everybody wanted to help and make it happen … It was like coming full circle, going back there”

Because of the personal diaspora Ang Lee faces and the international influences he draws upon, Lee’s films fail to accurately display many cultures instead giving them westernized and stereotypical features.

While the wuxia elements of Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon clearly derive from East Asian culture, many elements were changed slightly to appeal to international audiences. The emphasis on female characters hides the imbalance of gender roles within China. The English subtitles also lose cultural references in the original script.
